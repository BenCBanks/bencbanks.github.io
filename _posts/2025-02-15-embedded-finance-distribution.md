Embedded finance has a distribution problem disguised as a technology problem.

The infrastructure layer is largely solved. Banking-as-a-service providers, card issuers, and lending APIs exist. You can stand up a branded debit card or a lending product in weeks. The technology is not the bottleneck.

The bottleneck is distribution — and specifically, whether the platform embedding the financial product has the right customer relationship, the right data, and the right compliance posture to make it work.

Consider the pattern that actually succeeds: Shopify Capital, Uber's driver earnings products, Toast's restaurant lending. In each case, the platform has dense transactional data on the end customer, a captive audience with high switching costs, and a natural reason to offer the financial product. The loan or the card is not a new product — it is an extension of an existing relationship.

The platforms that struggle are the ones treating embedded finance as a revenue diversification play without the underlying data advantage. They are trying to compete with banks on banking, which is a hard game.

The right frame is not "how do we add financial services?" It is "what financial problems do our customers have that only we are positioned to solve?"
